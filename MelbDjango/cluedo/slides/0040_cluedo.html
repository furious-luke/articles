But for those who perhaps aren't so familiar, let's start with a brief refresher.

Cluedo

The Classic Mystery Game

Invented by a British pianist, the game is set as a murder mystery which takes place at a fancy dinner party. The game was originally called Murder, but Hasbro Entertainment thought that a little provocative, and thus they chose Cluedo, a portmanteau of the words Clue and Ludo.

The main instruments of the game are three decks of cards, the first of which is the suite of suspects. There are cards to represent all of these characters, including the notorious Colonel Mustard, who, of course, is the namesake of my persona this evening.

Note that each of these characters involves a color. This is to match the player tokens that are moved around the playing board.

At the beginning of the game each of these decks are independently shuffled. Without any player seeing the faces of the cards, one from each deck is chosen at random and concealed within an envelope.

This envelope is placed at the center of the board. All remaining cards are now shuffled together, and dealt evenly to the players, forming the playersâ€™ hands. Play then commences in a turn by turn basis, where each player attempts to be the first to deduce the content of the central envelope. This is achieved by asking judicious questions of their fellow players.

Professor Plum ...

... in the library ...

... with the candlestick!

When ready, players may make a guess in the form of the famous phrase which goes something like: "Professor Plum, in the library, with the candlestick". If they are correct, they have won. But if they chose poorly, they forfeit any chance to guess again and are effectively eliminated from the game.

Every game has a particular skill required of the players to win. In Poker one has to be able to bluff. Backgammon is a combination of strategy and luck. And Monopoly... well, in Monopoly you have to be prepared to lose all of your friends and family if you want to win.

But in the world of Cluedo, the victor is decided principally by the one who takes the best notes. This game is all about note taking. Players come up with vast and comprehensive sheets in order to gain an edge. I found this particular sheet on an individual's Cluedo fan site, and comes with the catch phrase: "Beat and enrage your family and friends through logical deduction and unashamed nerdiness". I think that sums Cluedo up well, and will do nicely as a conclusion to this section.
