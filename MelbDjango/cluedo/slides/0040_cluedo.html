At the beginning of the game, each deck is shuffled, and one of each category of card is selected at random, and in secret, and concealed in an envelope. This is the murder, and it is the player's responsibility to deduce the contents of the envelope.
By moving their player token from room to room, on their turn players may ask questions of their opponents, gradually forming theories about the trio of cards in the envelope.

Every game leverages different skills. Some games need the ability to bluff one's opponents, in others luck is the greatest ally. In the game of Cluedo, the winner is determined by one's ability to take notes. Advanced note taking strategies have been devised by many players, a testament to how geeky Cluedo is, and why I love it so.
